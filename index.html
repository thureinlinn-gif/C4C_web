About

        Health care challenges can be related to environmental factors in several ways. Environmental factors such as air pollution, water pollution, and exposure to hazardous chemicals can cause or exacerbate a range of health conditions, including respiratory and cardiovascular diseases, cancer, and neurological disorders. Health care workers and patients may be particularly vulnerable to these environmental hazards, especially if they are working or living in areas with poor air quality or contaminated water sources.

        Additionally, the environmental impact of health care facilities and operations can contribute to climate change and other environmental challenges. For example, health care facilities are responsible for a significant portion of greenhouse gas emissions and generate large amounts of waste, much of which is hazardous and requires special handling and disposal.

        Addressing these environmental factors and reducing their impact on health care workers and patients can help improve health outcomes and reduce health care costs. This can be achieved through measures such as improving indoor air quality, reducing energy use and emissions from health care facilities, and promoting sustainable practices throughout the health care system.

Mission

        To assist with that change, a new Carbon Footprint Calculator has arrived.

How to Tackle Carbon Emissions Ourselves

        Around the world, the concept of carbon footprint become widely known and seriously considered as the exposure of climate change is affecting influently our daily lives. The carbon footprint is the total combination of the greenhouse emissions that happen either for a commodity to be manufactured or for an acttion to occur. Not only several countries but also Myanmar government are commencing the carbon emission reduction plans and implementing their schemes into actions(Mulvaney, 2022).

        Myamar has provided its the Nationally Determined Contributions (NDCs) to restrict emissions of carbon footprints for the signing the Paris agreement in 2017(Myanmar at risk from worsening climate crisis - myanmar, 2019).Under the agreement, Myanmar has to reduce emissions, adapt to accomodate the influences of climate change,protect its current forests, biodiversity, invest in clean low-carbon electricity, restrict global warming "well below 2 degrees [Celsius] above pre-industrial levels while attempting to limit the increase to 1.5 degrees"(Mike, 2020). However, as a poor and developing country, Myanmar government has to implement an action for environmental commitments in accordance with the national budget of the country. Hence, it would be better to actually initialize with the help of international community. However, such contributions will take a substantial amount of time and steps. Thus, alternative methods would be the options.

        In Yangon, one of the most polluted city in Myanmar, the current air quality is 162(Air quality index) or unhealthy(Air pollution level). There are about 7 million people in Yangon where it is the most populated place and the center of merchandization. Due to the center of the Myanmar economy, aggregations of industries, automobiles, transportation, vessels, waste, utilizations of electricity trigger a huge amount of pollution and carbon emission(Yangon Air Quality Index (AQI) and Myanmar Air Pollution,n.d).

        One of the main reasons is vehicle emissions since the whole population employ a considerable number of cars, motorbikes, and vehicles which are mostly using on lower quality fuels and diesel fuel rather than their pristine correspondant resources which are at an internationally recognized level. Such fuels make higher levels of carbon emissions.In addition to that, Trucks and lorries , heavy weight automobiles in charge of carrying containers, goods, and other business uses are also using diesel. Since constricted places and policy to assess the age of the engines, fuels, and the vehicles are less found, old-fashioned applications also become an additional factor in increasing carbon emissions(Yangon Air Quality Index (AQI) and Myanmar Air Pollution,n.d).

        Emissions from factories are also to taken into account. As there are less an essential amount of development, implementations, and infrastructure which are under the international standardized level, it is also arduous to check crabon emissions from factories which utilize several amounts of fossil fuels, coals to produce the energy and commdoities. Then, the by-products of production are toxic contaminants and dangerous chemicals waste which reach to the atmosphere and flow into the rivers and seas. Furthermore, burning of fossil fuels for electricity, trushes and organiz waste materials, famlands, lower quality of contructions are adding to this carbon emissions(Yangon Air Quality Index (AQI) and Myanmar Air Pollution,n.d).

        As a result, the amount of heat rises up due to the incrasing amount of carbon in the atmosphere which traps the heat from returning back to the outside of the earth surface. Consequently, there are several incidents and effects of climate change. According to The historian U Thant Myint-U, grandson of former United Nations Secretary-General U Thant, “the impact of climate change on Myanmar will be nothing less than catastrophic.” In 2019, the monsoon season, more than 100,000 people had to escape fro their places and over 70 inhibitants departed from their lives because of a landslide and flooding affected by severe rains in Mon State(Myanmar at risk from worsening climate crisis - myanmar, 2019).

        There are alternative options to reduce carbon emissions such as Carbon Footprint Tax for Transportation, recycling waste into a new reusuable energy, Nuclear energy to replace burning fossil fuels.

        Among them, carbon taxes also known as carbon pricing is popullarly acknoledged as the most worthwhile method to decrease carbon emissions. A number of countries that implement carbon pricing and taxes including enlarging its current ones. For example, Sweden is one of the first leading countries to implement carbon pricing schemes successfully. Further, the Swedish carbon tax is the higher throughout much of the world with a price of US 123 dollar per tonne fossil carbon dioxide produced. In the Swedish energy taxation system, there are a carbon tax and an energy tax. The carbon tax was begun in 1991. The tax reform significantly reduced the marginal income taxes on both capital and labor, while at the same time expanding the base of the value added tax, among other changes. many people and business organizations can accomodate to the tax system due to the exchange system. The revenues from increasing the carbon tax offer contributions to the people alternatively by reducing general taxes in other sectors in order to also be gratified for lower income civilians(Schiebe, 2019).

        All in all, the Swedish instance indicates that it is feasible to eliminate decreasingly carbon emissions whilst the ecominy growth is sustaining. Between 1990 and 2017, local carbon emissions lessened by 26 percentage. Meanwhile, the Swedish Growth Domestic Product developed by 78 percentage. On top of that, Sweden became the 8th country on the Global Competitive Index. in 2019. Infact, the 26 percentage emissions lessening is predominantly affiliated with the elargement of fossil free electricity manufacturing such as nuclear energy, hydropower, and bioenergy. Conversely, the considerable enlargement of the district heating system, which is essentially powered by household waste and diverse wood leftovers, has almost completely eradicated the applications of fossil fuels for heating reasons in Sweden(Schiebe, 2019).

        The exemplification of the action of Sweden would be the best example for Myanmar citizens and government to learn how to encounter similar challenges.Nevertheless, such helpful applications are challenging for Myanmar where a significant amount of financial resources are required to tackle carbon footprint problems. This is because additional tax in Transportation increase the price of commodities, tansportation, and other financial challenges. That might decrease the Growth Domestic Product(GDP) of the country. Additionally, an advanced technology and resources from international scientits and engineers will be expensive. Moreover, even developed and affluent countries have to invest billions of dollars to build nuclear plants for producing electricity. Thus, it is hugely challenging to apply nuclear energy in Myanmar. Besides, utilizations of nuclear energy affect terribly the enviornments due to radioactive waste.
